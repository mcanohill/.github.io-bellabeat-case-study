Bellabeat Case Study - Consumer Insights for Marketing Strategy

Mary Sue Cano-Hill

2025-05-17

Objective

Understand user behavior patterns with smart fitness devices to improve Bellabeats marketing strategy.

Key Questions

  • Which features do users engage with the most?
  • Are there seasonal/daily/weekly trends in usage?
  • How can these patterns inform marketing messaging?

Share

ggplot(log_summary, aes(x = reorder(Dataset, -Total_Entries), y = Total_Entries, fill = Dataset)) +

heatmap_data <- steps_v2 %>%
  mutate(Weekday = lubridate::wday(ActivityHour, label = TRUE)) %>%
  group_by(Hour, Weekday) %>%
  summarise(avg_steps = mean(StepTotal), .groups = "drop")

ggplot(heatmap_data, aes(x = Hour, y = Weekday, fill = avg_steps)) +
  geom_tile(color = "white") +
  scale_fill_viridis_c(option = "C") +
  labs(title = "Average Steps by Hour and Day of the Week",
       x = "Hour of Day", y = "Weekday", fill = "Avg Steps") +
  theme_minimal()

Findings and Recommendations

This bar graph shows that heart rate was the most frequently logged data with over 1 million users.
  • Users used FitBit to log and track heart rate and sleep the most.
  • Marketing strategies can gear more towards the tracking of heart rate and sleep.
This heatmap shows that activity peaks in the evenings of the middle of the week.
  • Users were most active during the hours of 10 AM through 8 PM, with activity peaking during the hours of 7 PM and 8 PM.
  • Marketing can gear more towards evening walkers.
